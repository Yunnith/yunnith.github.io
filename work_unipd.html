Padova University: Dots Connection Test Web

Website created with JavaScript to conduct tests for a psychology research.

  • Role: UX/UI Designer & Programmer
  • Type: Research Web Platform
  • Year: 2025
  • Web prototype programmed: Dots Connection Test

Overview

This website was created to support psychological research conducted by a student and a professor of psychology at the University of Padua. They administered paper-based dot-connection tests to other students.

Problem & Goals

The paper-based format caused accuracy issues: completion times were not precise, students occasionally skipped dots, and errors required repeating the test. Data transfer to CSV format was also time-consuming.

The goals were to create a product capable of being accurate in calculating completion times, avoid common errors made by students and minimize their impact on the results, facilitate data collection in CSV format, and open the possibility of sending these tests to universities in other countries to collect data.

Process

Research & Observation

The first step was to meet with the researchers to discuss and understand what kind of data was important and why. Additionally, observing a test in progress allowed me to see and confirm which key information needed to be obtained and to identify some of the issues that were occurring.

I requested all the different test models with the name of each of the coordinates (the dots) and started working on how the website would be organized, what information would be displayed and what it would look like.

Identifying Pain Points

To better understand what problems already existed, the possible problems that could appear, the difficulties that users could present and all relevant information before designing the website, I made an affinity diagram.
I also started looking for references in tests, games, and similar media to see what characteristics they had in common and why.

Information Architecture & Flow

Despite the small number of pages on the website, I created a flow diagram to ensure the navigation was complete and that no step was missing from the beginning to the end of the entire journey.

Wireframing

After separating each note into possible groups, I started working on some sketches and the first wireframes of the website.
I also started noting what texts would appear and how, the distribution of each element, which elements were necessary and which were best to avoid, and so on.

Visual System & Interface Design

At this point I began to look for colors to use: I selected a restrained color palette to reduce visual fatigue while incorporating the University of Padua’s characteristic red for contextual consistency.
Once that was done, I started designing the website in digital format to get a better idea of ​​the result.

Development & Logic Implementation

I started programming the website in HTML and CSS to begin shaping the final product. Once everything was set up, I started programming with JavaScript, adding animations and other elements.

Final Outcome

You can access the website here: Dots Connection Test

The final system eliminated timing inaccuracies, prevented skipped-dot errors, and automatically generated CSV-ready datasets, significantly reducing administrative work for researchers.

Reflection

Working on this project allowed me to experience how design directly impacts research accuracy. Seeing how a small interaction detail could influence data quality made me approach every decision more carefully.

I’m especially proud of transforming a manual process into a system that supports both researchers and students. In future iterations, I would focus on expanding accessibility and testing the interface in different environments.
